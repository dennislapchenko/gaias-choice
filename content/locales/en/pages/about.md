We didn't set out to run a review site. We set out to travel.

We are Lidia and Denis — a creative couple with Russian roots, born and raised
in Latvia. We spent two years building our campervan from scratch, and now we
live on the road with our baby, born in early 2025, and our two Basenji dogs,
Kofik and Zlatka.

A van with a baby is a small, closed room. Everything in it — the bedding, the
bottles, the soap — shares the air your child sleeps in. That's what made us
care, hard, about gear that is **actually natural**: no hidden plastics, no
mystery fragrances, no synthetic coatings. And the same gear, it turns out,
makes every kind of trip easier — camper, car, plane, or a night at a friend's.

![Life on the road](/images/about.webp)

**Gaia's Choice** is where we share what we learn: honest reviews of gear that
survives this life, free courses on the natural living we practice (the
[Compass](/compass)), and a handwritten [Journal](/journal) from the road.
We're at the beginning and say so plainly — everything appears here only as we
actually buy it, pack it, practice it, and live it.

The road itself is a search: for a place to anchor, build an off-grid home,
grow food forests, and be part of a kind, sustainable community. "Buy less,
buy better" isn't a slogan for us — it's how a van, and one day a homestead,
actually work.

## What we test for

- **Materials** — organic cotton, wool, wood, glass, stainless steel over plastic.
- **Safety** — fragrance-free and non-toxic, because a car, tent, or camper is small-room air.
- **Durability** — if it can't survive road vibration and a toddler, it's out.
- **Footprint** — repairable, refillable, or compostable wherever possible.

We only recommend things we've used. If something disappointed us, we say so.

> The goal isn't to buy more. It's to buy once, buy well, and travel light.
