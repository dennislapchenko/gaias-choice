We didn't set out to run a review site. We set out to travel.

Seven months ago we moved into a camper with our baby and started driving.
Somewhere between a mountain pass and a laundromat parking lot, we realized how
hard it was to find gear that was **actually natural** — no hidden plastics, no
mystery fragrances, no synthetic coatings you'd rather not have next to a
sleeping infant in a small, closed space. It turned out the same gear made
every kind of trip easier — camper, car, plane, or a night at a friend's.

So we started keeping notes. What lasted. What off-gassed. What was worth the
weight and the shelf space when every centimeter counts.

![Life on the road](/images/about.webp)

**Gaia's Choice** is those notes, cleaned up and shared.

## What we test for

- **Materials** — organic cotton, wool, wood, glass, stainless steel over plastic.
- **Safety** — fragrance-free and non-toxic, because a car, tent, or camper is small-room air.
- **Durability** — if it can't survive road vibration and a toddler, it's out.
- **Footprint** — repairable, refillable, or compostable wherever possible.

We only recommend things we've used. If something disappointed us, we say so.

> The goal isn't to buy more. It's to buy once, buy well, and travel light.
