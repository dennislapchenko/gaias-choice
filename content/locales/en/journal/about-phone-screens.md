## What we thought the phone would just do quietly in the background

The plan, in a line or two — when you meant to just check the phone for a
minute, what you expected that glance to cost, what the evening was supposed
to look like before the screen got involved.

## What the phone screen actually did to that hour

The real version. Concrete beats adjectives: the hour, the room, the one
detail you still remember — the sound it made, the face lit up by it. If the
baby's rhythm rewrote the plan around that glowing rectangle — it usually
does — say so plainly; that rhythm is often the real protagonist.

## The moment the screen won

The moment it got difficult — the fight over the phone, the guilt, the time
that disappeared. Name it out loud — scared, tired, ashamed of how long it
had been — naming the fear first is what makes the rest believable. Then what
you tried.

## What got the phone back down (or what we'd change)

The way out, or the thing you'd do differently next time you feel the phone
pulling everyone's eyes. One honest lesson is enough — and it's allowed to be
"we still don't know how to put it down".

## One rule about the phone worth keeping

A boundary that earned its place, a small trick that actually worked, a
phrase you now say out loud — anything a reader could actually use. Leave
them something, the way you'd send a friend home with a jar.

## How we actually handle phone screens at home

The practice in plain words — and what it replaced. One honest line on why
most families do it differently is welcome; no preaching needed, the doing
is the argument.

## Where our phone-screen rule came from

Approaches have origin stories too: the day the phone caused a real problem,
the person or the article that made you rethink it. Start there — the story
is what makes the rest land.

## An ordinary evening with the phone put away

Concrete beats theory: the hour, the routine, the actual objects instead of
the screen, what the baby does meanwhile. Show it working on a normal
Tuesday, not an ideal one.

## What keeping the phone screen out of reach costs us

Everything real has a price — the missed message, the boredom, the moments
we gave up scrolling to be fully there instead. Name it; the named cost is
what makes the recommendation believable.

## If you want less phone screen in your evenings

The smallest honest first step, and the one mistake we'd warn a friend away
from. Leave the reader somewhere to start.
