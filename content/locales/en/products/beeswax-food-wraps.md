Cling film is exactly the kind of single-use plastic that piles up fast in a
tiny bin you have to carry to a dump station yourself.

A set of three **organic cotton + beeswax** wraps replaced ours completely. Warm
them with your hands, press around a bowl or half an avocado, and they hold.

## What we like

- **Zero waste** — reusable for about a year, then compostable at end of life.
- **No plastic, no fragrance** — just cotton, beeswax, jojoba oil, tree resin.
- **Space-saving** — folds flat; no bulky roll rattling in a drawer.

## Honest limits

They don't like heat, so no dishwasher and nothing straight from the stove.
And they won't wrap raw meat — for that we use a sealed steel container.

> A small learning curve, then you stop thinking about cling film entirely.
