Babies stay where you put them. Toddlers do not. When your little one starts
moving, the gear that matters shifts — here's the natural-first checklist we wish
we'd had.

## Food on the go

- Stainless bento and a collapsible cup — drop-proof and plastic-free.
- Beeswax wraps for half-eaten snacks that need to survive a bag.
- A bamboo or stainless spork that won't crack underfoot.

## Sleep anywhere

- A merino sleep sack so unfamiliar rooms don't mean cold or overheated nights.
- One familiar organic muslin — the smell of home helps in a strange place.

## Mess control

- Organic cotton washcloths instead of single-use wipes; rinse and re-use.
- One fragrance-free castile soap for hands, spills, and floors alike.
- A small wet bag in natural fabric for the inevitable soggy laundry.

## The mindset

You will not pack perfectly, and that's fine. Aim for **natural where it touches
skin, food, or sleep**, and let the rest be whatever gets you out the door.

> A moving toddler rewards a short, forgiving kit over a perfect, heavy one.
