The secret to easy travel with a baby isn't more gear — it's fewer things that
each do more. Here's the framework we use to pack a natural capsule that fits any
kind of trip.

## The rule of three uses

Before anything goes in the bag, it has to earn its place with **at least three
uses**. A muslin swaddle is a blanket, a sunshade, and a nursing cover. Castile
soap is body wash, dish soap, and floor cleaner. If it only does one thing, it
usually stays home.

## The natural family capsule

- **Sleep** — one merino sleep sack, two organic muslin swaddles.
- **Feeding** — glass bottles with sleeves, one stainless bento, a collapsible cup.
- **Care** — one fragrance-free castile soap, organic cotton washcloths.
- **Kitchen** — beeswax wraps instead of cling film, a bamboo brush.

## Why natural helps you pack lighter

Natural, multi-use items tend to be the ones that replace three plastic
single-taskers. Packing natural and packing light usually turn out to be the
same decision.

> Whether home is a camper, a hotel, or grandma's spare room, the capsule
> doesn't change — only the roof over it does.
