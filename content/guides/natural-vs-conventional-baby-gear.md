"Natural" is a marketing word as often as it's a real one. Here's how we
actually decide, so you can skip the greenwashing.

## Where natural materials clearly win

- **Anything against skin** — sleepwear, swaddles, wipes. Organic cotton and
  wool breathe and skip the fragrance finishes.
- **Anything near food** — stainless and glass don't leach or hold smells the
  way plastic does in a hot car.
- **Air quality** — in a small, closed space, low-VOC and fragrance-free is a
  real comfort difference, not a slogan.

## Where conventional is fine

- **Structural safety gear** — a car seat's job is crash protection. Buy the
  safest one that fits your vehicle; material ideology comes second here.
- **Short-lived items** — if something's used for two weeks and passed on,
  durability matters more than material purity.

## A simple rule

> Prioritize natural where it touches skin, food, or the air you breathe.
> Prioritize function where it protects your child in an emergency.

Spend your money and your limited packing space where the material actually changes
the outcome — not where a label just sounds nice.
