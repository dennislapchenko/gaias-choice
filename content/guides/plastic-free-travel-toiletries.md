Travel-size toiletries are a plastic trap: tiny bottles, big waste, and usually
a wall of synthetic fragrance in a bag you'll open in a small space. Here's what
we swapped to instead.

## The swaps that stuck

- **Solid shampoo & soap bars** — no liquids, no leaks, no plastic bottles, and
  they clear airport security without a second look.
- **Refillable tins** — decant a fragrance-free balm or sunscreen into a small
  steel tin instead of buying travel minis.
- **Bamboo toothbrushes** — compostable handle, and they don't shed microplastic
  into the sink.
- **A single castile soap** — see our review; it quietly replaces half this list.

## What to watch for

"Natural" bars can still hide fragrance — check for genuinely **unscented**
options, especially anything a baby will touch. And let bars dry between uses so
they last; a small draining tin makes a big difference on the road.

> Solid, refillable, unscented. Three words that cut most of the plastic and all
> of the leaks out of a travel wash bag.
