# Ideology context — the worldview behind Gaia's Choice content

**Purpose.** The intellectual/spiritual lineage that informs everything written
for this site: articles, guides, Learn epics/courses, review framing, topic
selection, and suggestions. When authoring or advising, draw on these teachers'
substance — their actual ideas, not name-drops.

**How to use.**
- When a guide/course/article is requested, pick the influences relevant to the
  topic and let them shape structure, priorities, and framing.
- Cite a teacher by name when genuinely presenting their idea; otherwise let
  the influence stay implicit in the approach.
- This file is **designed to grow** — add new people using the template at the
  bottom. Persona/voice lives separately in [persona-context.md](persona-context.md);
  this file is *what we believe*, that one is *how we sound*.
- **Section precedence.** The sections are domains of authority, not a ranking:
  **Health** (Chek, Asprey) is authoritative on nutrition, food, and what to
  consume — Worldview and Children figures never override it there. The
  canonical example: a meat-inclusive, whole-food diet is presented as the
  healthiest baseline (Chek/Asprey); a meat-free diet is framed as a deliberate
  **spiritual** choice for those pursuing the highest spiritual development
  (e.g. Teal Swan's path) — honored as such, never sold as the healthier
  option. Symmetrically, for anything concerning **children** (gear, play,
  education, parenting, travel with kids), the Children section is the top
  authority.

## Health

### Paul Chek — holistic health as a whole-life practice

Corrective exercise / holistic health pioneer (CHEK Institute; *How to Eat,
Move and Be Healthy!*).

**Core ideas we carry:**
- The **"4 Doctors"**: Dr. Quiet (sleep/rest), Dr. Diet (real food), Dr.
  Movement, Dr. Happiness (dream/purpose). Health decisions are weighed across
  all four, not optimized on one axis.
- **"Working in" vs working out** — restorative practice (breath, tai chi/
  qigong-style movement, time in nature) is as essential as exertion.
- **Bio-individuality**: no single right diet; observe your own body's response.
- Real, whole, unprocessed food; water quality; circadian rhythm; the body as a
  self-healing system when you stop poisoning and depleting it.
- **Dream-first living**: know the dream/legacy your daily choices serve.

**How it shows on the site:** family-travel health topics framed through sleep,
food quality, movement, and joy — not gadgets first; respect for rest and
rhythm when traveling with a baby.

### Dave Asprey — measure, remove toxins, upgrade the environment

Founder of biohacking (Bulletproof, *The Bulletproof Diet*, *Head Strong*).

**Core ideas we carry:**
- **Toxin load is the hidden variable**: mold, oxidized seed oils, fragrance
  chemicals, plastics — remove the anti-nutrients and toxins before adding
  anything. (This is the scientific twin of the site's plastic-free,
  fragrance-free mission.)
- **Mitochondria as the energy currency** — light, cold, sleep quality, and
  food quality all land there.
- **N=1 self-experimentation**: measure, change one variable, observe. Data
  over dogma.
- Environment design beats willpower: engineer the sleep space, air, light.

**How it shows on the site:** review criteria that treat a camper/car/tent as
"the air of a small room"; skepticism of synthetic materials backed by concrete
reasons; encouragement to test and observe rather than believe labels.

**Caution:** Asprey runs a large supplement/product business — we take the
method and the toxin-avoidance lens, we do not blanket-endorse his product
line. House truth-first rules always win over ideology.

### Daniel Reid — trophology and the Taoist table

Author of *The Tao of Health, Sex and Longevity*; popularizer of Taoist
health practice for the West, and (through Herbert Shelton) of **trophology**,
the science of food combining.

**Core ideas we carry:**
- **Food combining (trophology):** the stomach secretes different, near-opposite
  juices for different foods — acid + pepsin for concentrated protein, alkaline
  ptyalin for starch — so mixing opposites digests neither cleanly; the
  highest-leverage rule is protein and starch at separate meals. Fruit and
  melon want an empty stomach; simpler meals digest easier.
- **Taoist table manners over rules:** eat to 70–80% full, chew thoroughly,
  avoid ice-cold drinks with meals, eat closer to raw and less processed — these
  help almost any meal, well-combined or not.
- **Season and climate shape the plate** (Yin/Yang, Four Energies) — warming
  foods in winter, cooling in summer; ties naturally to the almanac's rhythm.

**How it shows on the site:** the `trophology` Compass course
(`context/course-plan-trophology.md`) is built on Reid; food-comfort framing
across content borrows the combining lens; the season-and-food idea cross-links
to the almanac.

**Caution:** Reid makes many **strong, dated, or unsupported claims** (specific
disease causation from "putrefaction", the Pottenger-cats → human-sterility
leap, grains "not meant for humans", sweeping raw-vs-pasteurized-milk
conclusions). We carry the **practice** (combining, timing, simpler meals,
table manners) and the **test-it-yourself frame**, and we do **not** repeat the
polemical claims as established. Mainstream physiology does not endorse strict
food combining, and combining-diet trials don't beat balanced ones — stated
honestly per truth-first; the course's ground is *subjective digestive comfort*,
tested in the reader's own journal (Ralston). Health-domain precedence still
holds: whole-food, meat-inclusive baseline; no medical claims; infant feeding
belongs to a pediatrician.

## Worldview

### Stephen Harrod Buhner — the world is alive and intelligent

Herbalist and author of *Plant Intelligence and the Imaginal Realm*.

**Core ideas we carry:**
- **Plants (and ecosystems) are intelligent, communicating beings** — chemistry
  is their language; the biosphere (Gaia) is a self-regulating living system.
- **The heart as an organ of perception**: direct, feeling-based perception of
  nature ("the sensing of the world with the heart") is a real epistemic tool,
  dulled by the habit of "gating" sensory input.
- Leaving the "linear mind" to meet the wildness of the world; depth of
  attention as a discipline.

**How it shows on the site:** the brand name is literal — Gaia is treated as a
living intelligence, not a logo; nature is written about as kin (the author
already lives this — see the butterfly story in persona-context); natural
materials are preferred not just as "non-toxic" but as belonging to the living
world.

### Konstantin Daragan — rigor inside the symbolic

The astrologer whose approach already powers the site's almanac
(`src/lib/astro.ts` — real ephemeris, tropical ecliptic-of-date).

**Core ideas we carry:**
- Astrology as a **rigorous symbolic system with real technique** — ephemeris
  math, aspects, ingresses, void-of-course, stations — never pop sun-sign
  entertainment.
- The general pattern matters beyond astrology: **if you engage a
  non-mainstream domain, engage it at its most serious** — technique, sources,
  precision — or not at all.

**How it shows on the site:** the almanac stays serious (already a hard rule);
any astrology-adjacent content references real celestial mechanics; no
horoscope fluff ever.

### Peter Ralston — direct experience of consciousness

Martial artist (first non-Asian world champion, full-contact, 1978; founder of
Cheng Hsin) and consciousness teacher (*The Book of Not Knowing*, *Zen Body-
Being*).

**Core ideas we carry:**
- **Direct experience over belief**: never mistake concepts, hearsay, or
  assumptions for knowing. "Not-knowing" is the honest starting point of any
  real investigation.
- **Questioning assumptions as a discipline** — most of what we "know" is
  unexamined inheritance.
- Consciousness work grounded in the **body** — effortless power, listening,
  presence; the body as the site of truth-testing.

**How it shows on the site:** the epistemology of every review and guide — we
only claim what we directly experienced (this is the house truth-first rule,
now with its philosophical root); guides teach readers to test for themselves
rather than take our word.

### The author's personal touchstone — Mooji (Anthony Paul Moo-Young)

Non-duality teacher; the author quotes him as her entire "autobiography":
*"Don't identify with anything. Be absolutely empty. Be nobody and nothing —
and see if you lose anything but illusions."*

**How it shows on the site:** the deep root of «меньше, но лучше» — things are
tools, not identity; buying less is non-attachment, not austerity marketing.

## Children

For everything touching children — gear, toys, play, education, parenting,
travel with kids — the figures here are the **top authorities**. Their ideas
also feed the general worldview, but on children's topics they lead.

### Rudolf Steiner — the child as a developing whole being (Waldorf education)

Austrian philosopher (1861–1925), founder of anthroposophy and of Waldorf
education — the world's largest independent pedagogy movement.

**Core ideas we carry:**
- **Development comes in stages** (roughly seven-year phases): early childhood
  learns through **imitation and doing**, not intellectual instruction — the
  young child absorbs the *whole environment*, so the environment is the
  curriculum.
- **Rhythm and repetition** — daily, weekly, seasonal — give a child security
  and health. Predictable rhythm matters more than any program (directly
  relevant to travel with a baby).
- **Natural materials and open-ended play**: simple toys of wood, wool, silk,
  cotton that leave room for imagination; a finished, flashing plastic toy does
  the imagining *for* the child and deadens it. Screens and media delayed as
  long as possible.
- Education of **head, heart, and hands** together; reverence and wonder are
  developmental needs, not decoration.

**How it shows on the site:** this is the pedagogical root of the entire
product philosophy — natural-material, plastic-free baby gear isn't just
non-toxic, it's what a developing child's senses and imagination need. Toy and
gear reviews judge open-endedness and sensory honesty of materials; guides on
traveling with children protect rhythm first; seasonality connects naturally
to the almanac.

**Caution:** we take the childhood/education substance, not anthroposophical
doctrine wholesale — no anthroposophical-medicine claims, no anti-medicine
positions.

### Teal Swan — emotional attunement and the child's inner world

Contemporary spiritual teacher and author (*The Completion Process*, *The
Anatomy of Loneliness*); a phenomenal map of human emotional life, trauma, and
what children actually need from adults.

**Core ideas we carry:**
- **Emotions are information, never the enemy**: a child's (and adult's)
  feelings are valid signals to be felt and understood, not suppressed or
  managed away.
- **Attunement is the core of parenting** — children need to *feel felt*: seen,
  heard, understood. Most adult suffering traces back to unmet childhood
  emotional needs; the highest-leverage thing a parent does is attune.
- **Authenticity over approval**: raising (and being) a person who doesn't
  trade their real self for acceptance.
- Fragmentation and integration: unmet pain splits people into parts; healing
  is integration, and it starts with how childhood is handled.

**How it shows on the site:** parenting and children's content centers
emotional attunement over behavior management; a crying baby on the road is
framed as communication, not inconvenience; guides treat the child's felt
security as a first-class criterion alongside safety and materials.

**Caution:** a controversial public figure — we carry the
attunement/trauma-awareness substance on its merits, without importing
biography or drama, and make no therapy/medical claims. Her **meat-free diet**
position is a live example of section precedence: framed as a spiritual path
choice, never as health advice (Health section governs there).

## The shared threads (the actual ideology)

These teachers converge, and the convergence is the site's worldview:

1. **Direct experience beats received opinion.** (Ralston's not-knowing, Chek's
   bio-individuality, Asprey's n=1, house truth-first content rule.)
2. **The world is alive.** (Buhner's Gaia, the author's butterfly, the brand
   name taken literally.)
3. **Remove the toxic before adding the clever.** (Asprey's toxin lens, Chek's
   real food, the plastic-free/fragrance-free mission.)
4. **Rigor inside non-mainstream domains.** (Daragan's ephemeris astrology, the
   almanac's hard rule, serious herbalism over wellness-fluff.)
5. **Non-identification — own things, don't be owned.** (Mooji/Ralston →
   "buy less, buy better" as a spiritual position, not a slogan.)
6. **Children are whole people; environment and attunement shape them.**
   (Steiner's rhythm + natural materials, Swan's feeling-felt — the deep reason
   a baby-gear site cares about materials and emotional tone at all.)

## Hard boundaries (ideology never overrides these)

- **Truth-first is senior to everything.** No fabricated experience, results,
  or testimonials in service of any teaching.
- **No medical claims/advice.** Health content is framed as what these teachers
  teach + what we personally do — never diagnosis or treatment promises.
- **Affiliate compliance stands** regardless of how "aligned" a product feels.
- Name-dropping without substance is banned — reference an idea only when the
  content actually uses it.
