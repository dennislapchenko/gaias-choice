## Course meta

- **Epic tag / slug prefix:** `herbalism` — files `herbalism-NN-<slug>.md`
- **site.yaml epic:** RU «Домашний травник» / EN "The home herbalist"
- **Spine artifact:** the **зелёная полка (green shelf) + травяной журнал** —
  a real shelf of self-made preparations that grows every chapter: first jar
  (ch. 1), infusion station (ch. 3), ten labeled herb jars (ch. 4), a tincture
  maturing (ch. 5), calendula oil/salve (ch. 6), harvest stores (ch. 8);
  ch. 11 completes and evaluates it against ch. 1's promise.
- **Stance:** Buhner leads the worldview (plants as whole, living, communicating
  chemistries), Ralston disciplines it (taste, observe, journal — «проверьте
  сами», never "believe us"). Scope = nourishment and gentle support of
  self-limiting complaints; red flags and interactions → doctor; babies →
  pediatrician first. Serious herbalism, zero wellness-fluff (Daragan
  principle). No medical claims.

## Ideology integration

- **Buhner** is the soul of the course: plants as living, whole, communicating
  beings; the ch. 2 sensing practice is his direct perception, framed strictly
  as «проверьте сами» (invitation, not doctrine); ch. 8 reciprocity is his too.
- **Ralston** disciplines it: taste-and-journal beats read-and-believe; the
  Summit teaches source evaluation and self-testing.
- **Children (Steiner, Swan)** lead ch. 6: warmth, honest natural materials,
  touch as attunement; child dosing conservative, babies → pediatrician.
- **Health (Chek, Asprey)** governs the terrain rule (ch. 9) and any
  food-adjacent claim: nourishing herbs are food-grade support, never a
  replacement for real food, sleep, movement.
- **Daragan** shapes ch. 8's seasonality (almanac cross-link) and the standing
  rule: serious herbalism or none.
- **Mooji/«меньше, но лучше»**: ten herbs in real relationship beat an
  apothecary of strangers.

## Editorial notes (kept small)

- Buhner's heart-perception has no scientific framework; the course presents
  it as a discipline of attention whose results land in the journal — the
  journal, not the doctrine, is the arbiter. (Stated as a small note in ch. 2.)
- Herb–drug interactions are real and understated in popular herbalism; ch. 7
  treats them as first-class safety content, not fine print.
