**The лестница доверия (four rungs — not a hierarchy).** «почувствовал сам»
(felt it yourself) · «давняя традиция» (long tradition) · «есть исследования»
(studies support it) · «маркетинговый миф» (marketing myth). The reader weighs
all four; «есть исследования» is a signal to weigh, **not a trump card** —
"science says" gets the same scrutiny as folklore (bought studies, dogmatic
Scientism). Direct verified experience (the journal) arbitrates. This is the
recurring epistemology device; it appears in every chapter.

## Domain non-negotiables (safety + honesty lines) WITH placement

1. **Synthetic fragrance = toxic, rejected fully; essential ("etheric") &
   absolute oils = a true natural product.** State the bright line in ch. 1;
   recall in ch. 9 (synthetic fragrance sold as "natural" = adulteration).
2. **доза решает — concentration is the safety story.** State in ch. 3; recall
   in every ch. 5 portrait, every blend (6, 7, 10), and dosing (8).
3. **Red lines — babies / pregnancy / pets get extra care, never casual
   territory; babies → pediatrician first.** Seed in ch. 3, formal list in
   ch. 8; recall in ch. 6 (child-safe calm = chamomile, not
   peppermint/eucalyptus), ch. 9 (internal use never casual for
   kids/pregnant/pets), ch. 10 (family blends). Children-domain precedence is
   binding — this is the one place the ladder's "both sides" does not soften
   the caution.
4. **Ingestion — honest, not dogmatic either way.** Seed in ch. 8, discussed
   fully in ch. 9. Default mode is inhalation (aroma-*therapy*) + diluted
   topical. A **known chemical-free, organic** oil was traditionally taken
   internally — so the course does **not** parrot a blanket "never ingest";
   it presents the practice honestly via the ladder, **discusses it, does not
   instruct it** (no casual protocols), and the «побудительные» critical-
   thinking notes cut both ways — against "drink it daily" MLM hype *and*
   against "never ingest" institutional dogma. Strong oils, children, and
   pregnancy get extra care regardless.
5. **Symmetric skepticism — "science says" gets the same scrutiny as tradition
   and marketing.** State in ch. 1 (the ladder is not science-on-top; direct
   experience arbitrates), model in ch. 2 (present the neuroscience, mark its
   gaps), sustain through 9 and 11. Grounded in Buhner (*Plant Intelligence*,
   closing chapters, on Scientism) and Chek (respect respectable science, go
   deep, verify against your own experience). No medical claims anywhere.
6. **Phototoxicity** (citrus oils + UV): state in ch. 5 (lemon portrait),
   formal in ch. 8.

## Editorial notes (kept small)

*(Owner-reviewed 2026-07-12 per epic-writing §8.)*

- **The fragrance tension is load-bearing (owner).** The site is
  *fragrance-free*; this course is about aromatic oils. Ch. 1 must draw the
  bright line explicitly — synthetic fragrance is toxic poison, rejected fully;
  essential and absolute oils are a true natural product — or the brand
  contradicts itself.
- **Ingestion is honest, not dogmatic (owner-corrected).** Inhalation is the
  default; diluted topical next; internal use of a known chemical-free organic
  oil was traditionally practiced and is presented honestly (not ruled out),
  via the ladder, discussed-not-instructed, with extra care for strong oils,
  children, pregnancy. Lives in ch. 9.
- **Symmetric skepticism (owner).** "Science-based" / "peer-reviewed" earns the
  same critical eye as tradition and marketing — often bought or dogmatic
  Scientism (Buhner; Chek). The ladder's «есть исследования» rung is a signal
  to weigh, not a trump card; the journal (direct verified experience)
  arbitrates.
- **Herbalism boundary + cross-linking.** Aromatherapy = the volatile fraction
  via smell + skin at trace doses; herbalism = ingest the whole plant. Managed
  by explicit framing + *earned* cross-links (now standing practice across
  Compass courses — link only where it deepens understanding), never repetition.
