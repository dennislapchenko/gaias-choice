## Ideology integration

- **Ralston** is the course's backbone: the journal is a discipline of direct
  experience — record, don't believe; failures get written down too.
- **Daragan principle**: engage the non-mainstream domain at its most serious —
  real sources (Hahnemann, Boericke, Kent), real technique, no wellness fluff.
- **Children (Steiner, Swan)** lead in ch. 6: rhythm as the child's baseline,
  attunement as the parent's observation instrument; babies → doctor first.
- **Health (Chek, Asprey)** governs ch. 8 entirely — remedy never substitutes
  terrain; food/sleep claims follow the Health domain, full stop.
- **Mooji/«меньше, но лучше»**: a 10-remedy kit known deeply beats a drawer of
  fifty half-known vials.

## Editorial notes (differing opinion, kept small per the owner's rule)

- The mainstream evidence position (major meta-analyses find no effect beyond
  placebo at high dilutions) is stated honestly in ch. 1 and revisited in
  ch. 11 — the course's answer is the Ralston frame (test, journal, judge
  yourself) plus hard safety boundaries, not a counter-claim. This is the
  condition under which the course stays truth-first.
- Home practice deliberately caps potency at 30C and scope at self-limiting
  complaints; anything chronic, anything in an infant, anything with a red
  flag belongs to professionals. The course says this repeatedly on purpose.
