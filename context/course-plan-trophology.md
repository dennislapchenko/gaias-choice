## Course meta

- **Epic tag / slug prefix:** `trophology` — files `trophology-NN-<slug>.md`
- **site.yaml epic:** RU «Трофология» / EN "Trophology" (entry after `herbalism`,
  before the EN-only `inside-websites`; art via `task mandalas`).
- **Spine artifact:** the **дневник пищеварения + «Карта стола»** — a real
  digestion journal (started ch. 1) and a one-page food-combining chart posted
  on the fridge with the family's own go-to meals rebuilt to obey the rules
  (built up ch. 2–4, finalized ch. 5). Ch. 5 evaluates both against ch. 1's
  promise.
- **Main influence:** Daniel Reid, *The Tao of Health, Sex and Longevity*
  (chapters "Diet and Nutrition / Mother Nature's Menu", "The Human Dietary
  Devolution", "Trophology: The Science of Food Combining"). Reid channels
  Herbert Shelton's *Food Combining Made Easy* and the Taoist Yin/Yang diet.
  Added to `context/ideology-context.md` under Health (2026-07-05).
- **Stance (truth-first, senior to everything — mirrors the homeopathy course):**
  we teach the combining discipline seriously (Daragan principle) as a practice
  to **test in your own journal** (Ralston), never as medical fact. The honest
  evidence note is in ch. 1 and revisited in ch. 5: mainstream physiology holds
  that a healthy gut digests mixed meals, and controlled trials have **not**
  shown food-combining diets beat calorie-matched balanced ones — the ground
  that survives honest scrutiny is **subjective digestive comfort**, which each
  reader tests on themselves (n=1: Asprey; bio-individuality: Chek). Reid's most
  dramatic claims (the Pottenger-cats → human-sterility leap, "5 lb of putrefied
  meat", grains "not meant for humans", milk/disease causation) are **flagged as
  the tradition's strong claims, not repeated as established** — no medical
  claims anywhere. Health domain (Chek, Asprey) governs all food claims: a
  meat-inclusive whole-food baseline stays the healthy default; meat-free framing
  is spiritual choice, not health advice (precedence rule). Anything for a baby →
  the child eats on their own rhythm; combining rules are for the adults' table,
  and a pediatrician leads on infant feeding.

## Ideology integration

- **Health (Chek, Asprey) governs** every food claim: whole-food, meat-inclusive
  baseline; bio-individuality and n=1 self-observation are the method, not dogma;
  toxin-load lens supports "eat it closer to raw / less processed".
- **Ralston** is the backbone: the journal is direct experience — record, don't
  believe; comfort is felt in *your* body, failures written down too.
- **Daragan principle:** engage the non-mainstream domain at its most serious —
  real sources (Reid, Shelton, the Taoist dietary canon), real mechanism, no
  wellness fluff — while stating the mainstream position honestly.
- **Mooji / «меньше, но лучше»:** simpler meals, fewer things on the plate — the
  plate is where non-attachment gets concrete (ch. 4's "simpler digests easier").
- **Children (Steiner, Swan):** the combining rules are the *adults'* discipline;
  a young child eats on their own rhythm and a pediatrician leads on infant
  feeding — stated in ch. 1 and ch. 5, never overridden.

## Editorial notes (differing opinion, kept small per the owner's rule)

- The mainstream evidence position — a healthy gut digests mixed meals, and
  food-combining trials don't beat balanced calorie-matched diets for weight —
  is stated plainly in ch. 1 and revisited in ch. 5. The course's answer is the
  Ralston frame (test subjective comfort in your own journal), not a
  counter-claim to physiology. This is the condition under which the course stays
  truth-first.
- Reid's most dramatic claims are deliberately **not** repeated as fact
  (Pottenger cats → human sterility; grains "not human food"; specific
  disease causation). We carry the *practice* (combining, timing, simpler meals,
  eat-closer-to-raw, eat till 70–80% full) and the *frame* (test it yourself),
  not the polemic.
